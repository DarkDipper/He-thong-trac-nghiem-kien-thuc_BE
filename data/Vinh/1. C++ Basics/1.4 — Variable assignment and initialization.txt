Assignment (<code>=</code>) is used to assign a value to a variable. Equality (<code>==</code>) is used to test whether two operands are equal in value.</p></div><p class="cpp-section cpp-topline" style="clear:both">Initialization</p><p>One downside of assignment is that it requires at least two statements: one to define the variable, and one to assign the value.</p><p>These two steps can be combined. Whether you use extra spaces for readability or not is a matter of personal preference.</p><p class="cpp-section cpp-topline" style="clear:both">Default initialization</p><p>When no initialization value is provided (such as for variable <em>a</em> above), this is called <span class="cpp-definition">default initialization</span>. In most cases, default initialization leaves a variable with an indeterminate value.
